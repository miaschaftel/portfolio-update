CHOW

Dietary restrictions shouldn't stop you from enjoying food.

Timeline:

5 weeks, Oct - December 2021

Roles:

Digital Product Designer, Product Manager

Skills:

UX/UI Design, Graphic Design, Sketching, Empathy Mapping, User Personas, Wireframing, Prototyping, Usability Testing

Tools:

Figma, Procreate, Pivotal Tracker

Problem Statement:

People who experience diagnosed or undiagnosed dietary restrictions or allergies need a place to track their meals and the corresponding symptoms to further understand any relationships so that they can create the best diet for themselves.

Description:

I came up with the idea for Chow after seeing a family member struggle with newfound dietary restrictions and allergies. The idea felt like the best and most simple solution, and proved to be so through User Testing. I sketched out my ideas and developed them into wireframes using Figma. I designed the high-fidelity prototype in Figma which is linked to the image above.

Chow provides a space for users to track meals and symptoms related to those meals. Throughout the development process, the product changed direction and narrowed down in scope. The product interprets relationships between certain foods and the symptoms tracked for that day or the related meal time. The value behind Chow is that it learns about the user within the first few uses until the algorithm understands the user’s eating habits and symptom frequencies. Using this information, Chow creates charts displayed on the homescreen so the user can get a summarized and interpretable view of their symptoms for the current week. As the user interacts with Chow more, the data will become increasingly accurate.

Process:

I began by sketching out ideas for the interface. My main focus was creating a platform that was simple and to the point with all the necessary functions. Since the user is likely using this to collect information about what foods cause them the most difficulty, I made sure to put the most useful functions on the homepage along with a chart of the week's data. These sketches show the early ideas I then adapted into wireframes.
